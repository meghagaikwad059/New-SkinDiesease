Skin Diseases

Melanocytic nevi

Melanocytic nevi are common moles formed by clusters of pigment cells. They are mostly harmless but should be monitored for changes.

Melanoma

Basal cell carcinoma is the most common skin cancer, often appearing as pearly or waxy bumps. It rarely spreads but can damage nearby tissues if not treated.

Nevus

A benign skin growth commonly known as a mole, usually pigmented and present from birth or acquired later.

Dermatofibroma

Dermatofibroma is a benign, firm, brownish skin nodule often caused by minor injuries. It is harmless and usually appears on legs or arms.

Actinic keratoses

Actinic keratoses are rough, scaly patches caused by sun damage and are considered precancerous.

Vascular lesions

Vascular lesions are abnormal blood vessel growths appearing red, purple, or blue. They include hemangiomas and angiomas and are generally benign.
